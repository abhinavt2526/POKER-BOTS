solving it case by case 

lets take 
number of persons (n) to be 5
and let k to be 3 
and x is the number of contributers

CASE 1 -> (x>k)
if x=4 ,4 people will contribute and all 5 will get reward 
now this will not have any  Pure Strategy Nash Equilibrium , because 
one person has is getting reward without even paying , someone always has an 
incentive to free ride , so this can never be a stable equilibrium

CASE 2 -> (x=k)
now x=k=3 , now this is a Pure Strategy Nash Equilibrium , becuase 
if someone who payed the fee and got reward think like to not pay , they 
will get into x<k category and then payout will 0 for everyone including him 
so its better to not move by some , if someone from not paying decides to pay 
then it becomes the case of x>k , so everyone still gets the same reward , so 
there is no benfit or profit by person who changed its decision. hence psne

CASE 3 -> (x<k)
 psne , because unless they x=k people contribute , nobody is gonna get 
anything , so there is no profit no loss case . 

SUMMARY :

There are two types of pure strategy Nash Equilibria:

Everyone stays out — the good never gets provided, a coordination failure
Exactly k people contribute — the good gets provided, and every contributor knows they're the deciding vote
