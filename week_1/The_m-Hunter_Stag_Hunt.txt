Part 1 : Everyone prefers 1/n of stag over hare

let's take n = 5 hunters,
 m = 2 (minimum needed for stag),
 k = 3 (threshold), 
 and x = number of stag hunters

CASE 1  (x > m)
say x = 4, so 4 people hunt stag and 1 hunts hare. stag gets caught, each stag hunter gets 1/4.
now the hare hunter thinks ,they can get better payoff if they switch ,
so they want to switch to stag. someone always has an incentive to join in. not stable, no PSNE

CASE 2  (x = n)
all 5 hunt stag, each gets 1/5. since everyone prefers even 1/5 over hare, 
nobody wants to leave and go catch a hare.
if you switch to hare ,you lose your 1/5 share and get a worse outcome. no reason to move.
therefore it is a psne 

CASE 3  (x < m)
say x = 1, one person hunts stag but you need at least m = 2. 
stag fails, that person gets nothing while hare hunters are fine.
that stag hunter immediately thinks they switch. not stable, no PSNE

CASE 4  (x = 0)
everyone hunts hare. if someone thinks of switching to stag alone, 
they still can't catch it (need m = 2). they just get nothing. no point switching, PSNE

Part 1 Summary
just two equilibria : 
everyone goes for hare, or everyone goes for stag
 nothing in between holds up.


Part 2 : Preference threshold k = 3

CASE 1  (x > k), say x = 4
4 people hunt stag, each gets 1/4. but since k = 3, they only accept shares of 1/3 or better. 
1/4 < 1/3, so it's worse than just catching a hare.no PSNE

CASE 2  (x = k = 3)
3 people hunt stag, each gets 1/3. this is exactly the threshold they're comfortable with.
a stag hunter thinks of leaving  if they leave, x = 2, stag still gets caught (since m = 2), but now
 they're getting hare instead of 1/3. they prefer 1/3 over hare. no reason to leave 

a hare hunter thinks of joining  if they join, x = 4, each gets 1/4. but 1/4 < 1/k = 1/3, 
so they prefer hare. no reason to join 

so its a psne 

CASE 3  (m <= x < k), say x = 2
now each will get 1/2 >1/3, so hare one will come and join , and make the 
thing worse for stag ones , no psne 

CASE 4 (x < m), say x = 1
only 1 person hunts stag but you need at least m = 2. stag fails completely. 
that hunter gets nothing, meanwhile everyone else has their hare. no psne

CASE 5  (x = 0)
everyone catches hare. someone thinks of going for stag alone but m = 2, 
so one person can't catch it. they'd walk away with nothing.
no point switching.  PSNE
